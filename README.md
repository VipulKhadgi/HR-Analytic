# HR Analytics: Promotion Delay & Employee Attrition Analysis

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing HR data to understand the relationship between **career growth, promotion delays, and employee attrition**.

The goal of this analysis is to identify why employees leave organizations and how delayed promotions impact retention. Using data analysis and visualization techniques, this project highlights key patterns in employee career progression and attrition behavior.

---

## Problem Statement

Employee retention is a major challenge for organizations. One of the key factors influencing employee satisfaction is career growth.

This project investigates:

- Does delayed promotion increase employee attrition?
- How does career progression affect employee retention?
- Are there specific time periods where attrition risk increases?

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Promotion Delays Increase Attrition Risk

The analysis shows that employees experiencing longer gaps without promotion have a higher probability of leaving the organization.

- Attrition spikes significantly after extended periods without career advancement.
- Employees with limited growth opportunities show higher turnover rates.

### 2. Career Progression is Directly Connected to Retention

Employees with better promotion opportunities are more likely to stay.

Career development acts as a major retention factor because employees value:

- Growth opportunities
- Recognition
- Increased responsibilities
- Professional development

### 3. Attrition Pattern Identification

The analysis identified specific intervals where attrition increases:

- Attrition rises between approximately **58%–77% after long promotion gaps**.
- Employees with stagnant career paths are at higher risk of leaving.
